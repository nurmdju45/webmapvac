Information about Vác

Vác, nestled in the Danube Bend, boasts a rich history spanning nearly a millennium.

The town owes its name to a hermit named Wach and King Géza I.

Recent archaeological excavations failed to uncover remains connected to Géza I.

A statue of King Géza I graces the castle wall overlooking the Danube.

Vác offers picturesque views and a rich cultural experience.
